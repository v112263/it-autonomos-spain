José Manuel has worked in immigration law since 2015 and processes all types of residence permits in Spain. He heads a law firm in Barcelona that serves both private clients and companies - including those relocating employees to Spain. Among the most common requests are Digital Nomad, the highly qualified specialist visa, and the Beckham tax regime.

José holds an official qualification that gives him the right to represent clients in court. If a residence permit is denied, he files the appeal himself: first with the immigration office, and if needed - takes the case to court without handing it off to an outside lawyer. At this stage, most specialists are forced to bring in a separate lawyer.

José personally reviews every document package before submission and addresses in advance the weak points that usually lead to refusals. He also advises on the tax side of relocation - when the Beckham regime is worthwhile and how changing countries affects tax residency.

His strength is two areas under one roof. José works together with gestor [Tatiana](#tatiana---gestor), so immigration and taxes can be handled in one place: once the residence permit is obtained, you can immediately register as autónomo or set up an SL, keep the accounting, and optimize taxes without searching for a separate specialist.
