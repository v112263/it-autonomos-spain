## Linking your account to a bank branch

At many Spanish banks they will confidently tell you that you can go to any branch for any matter. Below is
my personal example of why that's not the case - it might help someone save a lot of time and nerves.

This information will most likely stay relevant for a long time. In Spain every account is administratively tied to
its own branch, and for complex matters they'll send you right back there anyway.

When I registered as autónomo, I received my payments at Santander. Later, after [getting a mortgage](https://spainlifeguide.com/en/mortgage/){:target="_blank"}, I switched completely to CaixaBank and now receive payments there - it gives an additional discount on the mortgage. Meanwhile, I keep using Santander as a separate account, from which taxes and Seguridad Social payments are debited.

Even before all this, when I moved to a new place, I went to a Santander branch a minute's walk from
home. I came in and asked to move my account entirely to their branch, and to update my documents at the same time. They
updated my documents, but regarding moving the account the manager started actively talking me out of it. She said I'd only waste my time and there was no point - you can go to any branch for any matter, you can even get a cita anywhere without any problems. On top of that, if you move the account, it supposedly has to be closed and a new one opened. Intuitively I didn't like it, but I believed her and left everything as it was. As it turned out later - that was a mistake.

A couple of months later my account was frozen. I didn't get any notification - not in the app, not by mail, not by
SMS.
I only realized it when a transfer to this account didn't go through and got canceled. I called support, and they told me the account was
frozen and the issue could only be resolved at the branch. I went to the branch near my home, but the manager there
told me they couldn't solve my problem - I had to go specifically to the branch where I opened the account. And
it's in another city, about 40 minutes from home. With two kids - that's the dumbest logistics you could possibly
come up with. I quickly moved all my payments to my other account, so they'd be debited from it temporarily.

About a month later I finally made it to my branch and spoke with the director. It turned out that only he
has access to the letter that came about me from the AML department (anti-money laundering). No one else at the
bank can open this letter. He looked at it and said the complaint came up because of regular deposits into my
account (those very autónomo payments). And that's despite the fact that I'd provided all the documents in advance, several times even:
[Modelo 036](#modelo-036), [Vida Laboral](#vida-laboral), declarations, and a lot more. Before the trip I prepared,
just in case, [the whole document package](#documents-for-the-bank-after-autónomo-registration) - the annual declaration (renta), quarterly declarations, receipts for
Seguridad Social payments, proof of residence, and so on. He said everything was fine and the account would be unblocked within
a few days.

Then I asked whether, after it's unblocked, I'd be able to move the account to the branch near my home. He said the same thing
I'd already heard "there's no point moving it, you can go to any branch for any
matter," and only for such rare specific issues do you need to come to him. And if I switch to another branch, then
he won't be able to help me anymore, so it's better for me to stay at their branch.

In situations like this I always recall the thesis: if someone is convincing you that something is good for you, then it's good in
the first place for them. Banks really do have their own nuances. It's beneficial for branches to keep clients with clean, regular income, because it affects their metrics and bonuses. So a sensible branch director won't want to let go of an IT guy who pays taxes.

There were no problems with unblocking - all my income is 100% legal, and I pay taxes on everything. But I really did spend a lot of time and nerves on
all this hassle.

So when you move, transfer your account to the branch near your home right away. Don't listen to those who'll try to convince you that
there's no point and that any matter can be handled at any branch. And one more thing: some managers will
say that to move the account you need to close the old one and open a new one. It depends on the bank - at some the existing
account can simply be moved to another branch without opening or closing anything, at others it really won't
work. But sometimes managers say this just because they're lazy.
